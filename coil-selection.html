WHAT IS A COIL?

A heat-transfer device in an AHU/FCU that heats or cools air by exchanging heat with a fluid — chilled/hot water, steam or refrigerant.

INPUT PARAMETERS 📝

Cooling coil (SI) — edit any value
Air ρ = 1.2 kg/m³ · cp,air = 1.005 · cp,water = 4.186 kJ/kg·K. Enthalpies h₁, h₂ from the psychrometric chart.

3 WORKED EXAMPLE — COOLING COIL 🧮

Given
V̇ = 17,000 m³/h · Entering 27 °C DB · Leaving 13 °C DB Chilled water 7 °C (EWT) / 12 °C (LWT) · h₁ = 70, h₂ = 51 kJ/kg
Total capacity
Q = 1.2 × (17000/3600) × (70 − 51) = 1.2 × 4.72 × 19 = 107.7 kW (30.6 TR)
Water flow
V̇w = 107.7 / (4.186 × 5) = 5.15 L/s = 18.5 m³/h
Q ≈ 108 kW · 18.5 m³/h
Total capacity uses the enthalpy difference (Q = ρ·V̇·Δh). The original imperial sheet mistakenly used the sensible constant 1.08 in the total-capacity formula; this SI tool uses the correct enthalpy-based total heat.

4 TOTAL CAPACITY (Q) ❄️

Q = 1.2 × V̇ × (h₁ − h₂)
V̇ in m³/s = (m³/h)/3600 · ρair = 1.2 kg/m³ · Δh in kJ/kg → kW

5 SENSIBLE CAPACITY (Qs) 🌡️

Qs = 1.2 × 1.005 × V̇ × (T₁ − T₂)

5b LATENT & SHR 💦

QL = Q − Qs  ·  SHR = Qs / Q

6 COIL BY-PASS FACTOR 🔁

BPF = (T₂ − TADP) / (T₁ − TADP)
Lower BPF = better coil. Contact factor = 1 − BPF.

7 WATER FLOW RATE 🚰

V̇w = Q / (4.186 × ΔTw)
Uses total Q (water removes sensible + latent heat). ΔTw = LWT − EWT (°C); 1 L water ≈ 1 kg.

BASIC SELECTION STEPS 🧭

  1. Determine air-side entering / leaving condition
  2. Select coil type (CW / HW / DX / steam)
  3. Decide entering & leaving fluid temperatures
  4. Select coil face area (reference / trial)
  5. Check air-side & water-side pressure drop
  6. Check capacity
  7. Finalize selection

SELECTION CRITERIA ✅

  • Meets required capacity
  • Leaves air at the desired condition
  • Air-side pressure drop within limit
  • Water-side pressure drop within limit
  • System factor is acceptable

PRESSURE DROP & SYSTEM FACTOR 📉

  • Air-side pressure drop: ≤ 1.25 kPa (5 in.wg)
  • Water-side pressure drop: ≤ 30 kPa (≈3 m wg, 10 ft.wg)
If pressure drop is too high, select the next coil size. Apply a system factor on selection — air side ≈ 1.0–2.0, water-side performance ≈ 1.05–1.15 — based on manufacturer data, fouling factor & location constraints.
