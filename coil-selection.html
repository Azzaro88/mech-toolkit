WHAT IS A COIL?

A heat-transfer device in an AHU/FCU that heats or cools air by exchanging heat with a fluid — chilled/hot water, steam or refrigerant.

INPUT PARAMETERS 📝

Cooling coil — edit any value
Enthalpies h₁, h₂ are read from the psychrometric chart (entering/leaving air states).

10 WORKED EXAMPLE — COOLING COIL 🧮

Given
CFM = 10,000 · Entering 80°F DB / 67°F WB · Leaving 55°F DB / 54°F WB Chilled water 44°F (EWT) / 54°F (LWT) · h₁ = 30, h₂ = 22 Btu/lb
Total capacity
Q = 4.5 × 10,000 × (30 − 22) = 360,000 Btu/hr = 30 TR (105.5 kW)
Water flow
GPM = 360,000 / (500 × 10) = 72 GPM  (2.4 GPM/ton)
Q ≈ 30 tons · 72 GPM
The original sheet uses 1.08 instead of 4.5, giving Q = 86,400 Btu/hr and GPM = 12.96 — both far too low for a 10,000 CFM coil. The values above use the correct constants.

4 TOTAL CAPACITY (Q) ❄️

Q = 4.5 × CFM × (h₁ − h₂)
The source sheet writes 1.08 here, but 1.08 is the sensible (temperature) constant. For an enthalpy difference the correct air-side constant is 4.5 — this tool uses 4.5 so the result is realistic.

5 SENSIBLE CAPACITY (Qs) 🌡️

Qs = 1.08 × CFM × (T₁ − T₂)

5b LATENT & SHR 💦

QL = Q − Qs  ·  SHR = Qs / Q

6 COIL BY-PASS FACTOR 🔁

BPF = (T₂ − TADP) / (T₁ − TADP)
Lower BPF = better coil. Contact factor = 1 − BPF.

7 WATER FLOW RATE 🚰

GPM = Q / (500 × ΔTw)
Uses total Q (the water removes sensible + latent heat). ΔTw = LWT − EWT.

BASIC SELECTION STEPS 🧭

  1. Determine air-side entering / leaving condition
  2. Select coil type (CW / HW / DX / steam)
  3. Decide entering & leaving fluid temperatures
  4. Select coil face area (reference / trial)
  5. Check air-side & water-side pressure drop
  6. Check capacity
  7. Finalize selection

SELECTION CRITERIA ✅

  • Meets required capacity
  • Leaves air at the desired condition
  • Air-side pressure drop within limit
  • Water-side pressure drop within limit
  • System factor is acceptable

PRESSURE DROP & SYSTEM FACTOR 📉

  • Air-side pressure drop: ≤ 5 in.wg
  • Water-side pressure drop: ≤ 10 ft.wg
If pressure drop is too high, select the next coil size. Apply a system factor on selection — air side ≈ 1.0–2.0, water-side performance ≈ 1.05–1.15 — based on manufacturer data, fouling factor & location constraints.
