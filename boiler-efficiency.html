1 · WHAT IS BOILER EFFICIENCY?

The ratio of useful heat absorbed by the water/steam to the total heat supplied by the fuel.

2 BOILER HEAT FLOW DIAGRAM 🔥

Useful output = heat that ends up in the steam. Everything leaving on the right (flue gas, radiation/convection, blowdown) is a loss that lowers efficiency.

3 EFFICIENCY FORMULA 📐

η % = Heat Absorbed by Water/SteamHeat Supplied by Fuel × 100
  • Q₁ = m × (hs − hf)  heat into steam
  • Q₂ = mf × GCV  heat from fuel
  • η = (Q₁ / Q₂) × 100

4 CALCULATION DETAILS 📋

ItemHow to Calculate
Heat Absorbed by Water/Steam (Q₁) Q₁ = m × (hs − hf)
m = mass of steam generated (kg) · hs = enthalpy of steam (kJ/kg) · hf = enthalpy of feed water (kJ/kg)
Heat Supplied by Fuel (Q₂) Q₂ = mf × GCV
mf = mass of fuel consumed (kg) · GCV = Gross Calorific Value (kJ/kg)
Boiler Efficiency (η) η (%) = (Q₁ / Q₂) × 100

! IMPORTANT NOTES 📝

  • Use consistent units (kJ and kg).
  • GCV should be used for boiler efficiency calculation.
  • Losses reduce efficiency (flue gas, radiation, blowdown, etc.).
  • Higher efficiency means better fuel utilization.

★ WORKED EXAMPLE 🧮

Given
m = 1000 kg · hs = 2750 · hf = 250 mf = 120 kg · GCV = 40,000 kJ/kg
Steps
Q₁ = 1000 × (2750 − 250) = 2,500,000 kJ Q₂ = 120 × 40,000 = 4,800,000 kJ η = (2,500,000 / 4,800,000) × 100
Boiler Efficiency = 52.08 %
