WHY IT MATTERS

Knowing the filling time helps in planning operations, pump selection, scheduling and ensuring water availability.

2 OVERHEAD TANK 🛢️

Find the tank volume from its shape, then divide by the steady inflow rate to get the filling time.

3 FORMULA & KEY VARIABLES 📐

T = VQ × 60
  • VVolume of tank — cubic metres (m³)
  • QInflow rate — cubic metres / hour (m³/h)
  • TFilling time — Minutes (min)
  • HWater height — Metre (m)
NOTE: 1 m³ = 1000 L  ·  π (pi) = 3.1416 (approx.)
Q in m³/h → V/Q is in hours; × 60 → minutes.
Cylindrical volume: V = π × (D/2)² × H  ·  Rectangular: V = L × W × H

! TIPS 💡

  • Use the formula for any tank shape with known volume.
  • Regularly inspect valves, pipes and level controls for efficiency.

★ WORKED EXAMPLE 🧮

Given — Overhead Tank
D = 3.0 m · H = 2.5 m · Q = 45 m³/h
Step 1 — Volume
V = 3.1416 × (3/2)² × 2.5 V = 3.1416 × 2.25 × 2.5 = 17.66 m³
Step 2 — Filling time
T = (17.66 / 45) × 60 = 0.392 h × 60 = 23.55 min
T ≈ 23 minutes 33 seconds
