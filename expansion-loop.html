WHAT IT DOES

Sizes a U-shaped expansion loop so a straight pipe run can safely absorb its thermal growth without over-stressing — for process piping (carbon steel default).

EXPANSION LOOP LAYOUT ➿

Place the loop near the midpoint of the straight run, with anchors at the ends and guides directing growth into the loop legs.

1 THERMAL EXPANSION 🌡️

ΔL = α × L × ΔT
α (1/°C) · L (m) · ΔT (°C) → ΔL (m). Carbon steel α = 12×10⁻⁶ /°C.

2 LOOP LEG LENGTH 📐

L₁ = √( 2·E·D·ΔL / (3·S) ) (engineering / governing)
L₁ = C · D · √ΔL (empirical rule of thumb)
Units: E, S in N/mm² · D, ΔL in mm → L₁ in mm. The empirical C·D·√ΔL form (mm) is dimensionally loose and far more conservative — the guided-cantilever engineering formula governs.

QUICK DESIGN NOTES 🧭

  • Place the loop at the midpoint of the straight run.
  • Use bend radius ≥ 1.5D (good for CS pipe).
  • Provide proper anchors & guides as shown.
  • Verify stress in CAESAR II / AutoPIPE per ASME B31.3.

DESIGN STANDARD 🛡️

ASME B31.3 — Process Piping.
Always check flexibility, stress & support adequacy during final design. This tool gives a preliminary leg length only.

WORKED EXAMPLE 🧮

4" NB · D = 114.3 mm · L = 60 m · ΔT = 80 °C
ΔL = 12×10⁻⁶ × 60 × 80 = 57.6 mm
L₁ = √(2×200000×114.3×57.6 / (3×82.74)) = 3 257 mm
→ Required loop leg ≈ 3.3 m (each)
