2 POWER FORMULA 🧮

P = ρ · g · Q · Hηp (W)
Q m³/s · H m · ρ kg/m³ · g 9.81 m/s² · η decimal → W
Alternative forms (kW):
P = ρ·g·Q(L/s)·H / (1000 × 1000 × η)
P = ρ·g·Q(m³/h)·H / (3600 × 1000 × η)
For water: P(kW) = 0.00981 · Q(L/s) · H / η = 0.002725 · Q(m³/h) · H / η. (The source sheet's L/s coefficient 0.0981 is a 10× typo — corrected here.)

3 TOTAL HEAD (H) 📏

H = Hz + Hf + Hm  (m)
  • Hz static head — elevation difference between delivery & suction levels
  • Hf friction head loss in suction & delivery pipes
  • Hm minor losses in fittings & valves
Always include all losses in total head; use the Pipeline Sizing tool to get Hf from Darcy–Weisbach.

7 IMPORTANT POINTS ⭐

  • Higher-efficiency pumps need less power.
  • Power rises with both discharge (Q) and head (H).
  • Always include all losses in total head.
  • Select so the pump runs at its Best Efficiency Point (BEP).

8 STEPS SUMMARY 🧭

  1. Find discharge Q.
  2. Calculate all head: static + friction + minor losses.
  3. Find total pump efficiency.
  4. Add heads → total head H.
  5. Substitute into the power formula → P.
