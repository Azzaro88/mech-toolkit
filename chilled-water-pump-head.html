WHAT IS PUMP HEAD?

The total energy per unit weight the pump must add to the water to overcome system resistance and maintain the required flow.

1·2 PUMP HEAD FORMULA 💧

HT = Hs + Hd + Hv + He
  • Hs — Static head (m)
  • Hd — Friction head in pipes (m)
  • Hv — Velocity head (m)
  • He — Minor losses head (m) — fittings, valves, bends…
Unit of head: Meter of Water Column (mWC).

3 COMPONENTS OF HEAD 🧩

  • Static (Hs) — vertical height between suction & discharge levels.
  • Friction (Hd) — loss in straight pipe (Darcy–Weisbach).
  • Velocity (Hv) — Hv = v²/2g, g = 9.81 m/s².
  • Minor (He) — fittings, valves, bends, strainers, expansion joints.

◇ SYSTEM SCHEMATIC 🗺️

The pump overcomes all resistances and maintains the design flow rate.

4 FRICTION HEAD (Hd) — DARCY–WEISBACH ⚙️

Hd = f · (L/D) · v²2g
f friction factor · L pipe length (m) · D internal diameter (m) · v velocity (m/s) · g 9.81 m/s²
Friction factor (f)
  • Depends on Reynolds number (Re) & relative roughness (ε/D).
  • For practical design, use the Moody chart.
  • Re > 4000 (turbulent): typical f = 0.02–0.04.
  • For design, take f = 0.02 (common for steel pipe).

5 MINOR LOSSES (He) 🔧

He = ΣK · v²2g
K loss coefficient (from tables) · ΣK sum of all K · v velocity (m/s)

6·7 VELOCITY & DESIGN FLOW RATE ❄️

6 · Velocity
v = Q / A  ·  A = πD²/4
Q flow rate (m³/s) · A pipe cross-section (m²) · D internal diameter (m)
7 · Design flow rate from cooling load
Q (m³/hr) = Cooling Load (kW)1.163 × ΔT
ΔT = supply–return temperature difference (°C)
1 TR = 3.517 kW1 kW = 0.86 kcal/hr
